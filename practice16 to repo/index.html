About Me

My life has been filled with many obstacles. Yet after overcoming them, I am still able to maintain a sense of optimism for the future and a smile on my face.

Starting with my childbirth, I had a near death experience. My mom went into labor at home and I came out choking on the umbilical cord and had stopped breathing. Sheriff Deputy Mark Richardson saved my life. He also gave me my best friend, my bear, who is seen in the picture. My bear is my symbol of hope and pulls me through the struggles of my life.

Growing up, I was one of two kids in a family of four and we lived near the poverty line. We struggled to get by, and our family had enough just for rent and feed ourselves. I spent most my childhood at home playing with my bear and toys while using my imagination to keep myself entertained.

I entered college with the goal to obtain a better future for my family and myself. So I studied really hard and eventually graduated top of my department with a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering.

Unfortunately, about six months before my graduation, my mom passed away from sudden health complications. The pain from her loss motivated me to make a lifestyle change. I started to work out and eat healthier. Over the course of a few years I lost weight (about 20 pounds and 8 inches off my waist) and kept it off since.

After facing a near death experience at birth, growing up in poverty, losing my mother, and fighting obesity, I was finally free to pursue my passions. So during my leisure time, I taught myself to cook, photography, electronics design, and programming. I designed, built, integrated, and tested a second generation Autonomous Rover. The hardware is complete, and software development will start soon. I also taught myself various web technologies so I could create this website you see today.
