After graduating from Cal Poly Pomona, I joined the Northrop Grumman Corporation (NGC) at the site in Azusa, California as an Electronics Engineer.

I started my career on the Space-Based Infrared System (SBIRS) program where I tested flight hardware, conducted Test Readiness Reviews, redlined schematics for flight CCA to mitigate parts obsolescence and single point failures, and drafted cable interconnect drawings.

While still on the SBIRS program, I transitioned to the Special Test Equipment group to expand my knowledge base. There I the designed test cables and created interconnection drawings for payload test sets. I also helped with engineering checkout and hardware integration.

One day there was a call for volunteers to help the Exoatmospheric Kill Vehicle (EKV) program. I volunteered to go to Salt Lake City, Utah for a few months. There I was part of the design of their replacement test sets. I also helped with troubleshooting and engineering checkout.

When I returned to Azusa, I joined the DSP On-Orbit Support and Sustainment Contract (DOSSC) program as a Data Analyst where I did log file extractions, analyzed data, authored reports, and wrote various Unix/Linux scripts.

There was another call for volunteers to travel and resolve critical engineering issues on the Joint Polar Satellite System (JPSS) weather satellite program. I volunteered to travel to Hauppauge, New York for a month. Although I had a limited RF background, I quickly picked up and performed testing on Radio Frequency (RF) flight modules.

When I came back, I continued on with flight instrument testing, designed cables, and did the preliminary flight design for an in-rush current limiter. I also coordinated activities as the liaison between Production Control, Supply Chain Management, Subcontracts, Manufacturing, Mission Assurance and Team Leads for multiple Integrated Product Teams (IPTs).

After a successful delivery of the JPSS1 instrument, I decided I wanted to step outside of the space hardware world. Thus I recently I transferred to Northrop Grumman’s site in Baltimore, Maryland. I am excited to for the opportunity to be part of the Test Design Engineering department where I will be working with test equipment for radar systems.



Among many things, Northrop Grumman is well known for creating the B-2 Stealth Bomber for the U.S. Air Force, one of the nation’s most powerful, most survivable, and most valued aircraft.
